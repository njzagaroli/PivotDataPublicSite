Warehouse Solutions

The Data Warehouse is a subject-oriented, integrated, time-variant, and non-volatile collection of data that supports the decision making process. This database is kept separate from the organization's operational databases or applications. It contains consolidated historical data which helps the organization to analyze its business. The Warehouse helps the executives to organize, understand, and use data to make strategic decisions.

Why the Data Warehouse is separated from Operational Databases

  • The operational database is constructed for well-known tasks and workloads, such as searching particular records, indexing, etc., but the data warehouse queries are often complex, and it presents the general form of data.
  • Operational databases support the concurrent processing of multiple transactions. Concurrency control and recovery mechanisms are required for operational databases to ensure robustness and consistency of database.
  • Operational database queries allow the reading of, and modification of operations, while the OLAP query needs just read-only access to stored data.
  • Operational databases maintain the current data, on the other hand, data warehouses maintain the historical data.

Data Warehouse Features

The key features of the Data Warehouse:
  • Subject Oriented - The Data Warehouse is subject oriented, because it provides us the information around a subject rather than the organization's ongoing operations. These subjects can be portfolios, investments, relationships, performance, etc. The Data Warehouse does not focus on the ongoing operations, rather it focuses on modeling and analysis of data for decision making.
  • Integrated - The Data Warehouse is constructed by integration of data from heterogeneous sources such as partnership accounting systems, general ledgers, custodians, relational databases, flat files, etc. This integration enhances the effective analysis of data.
  • Time-Variant - The data in the Data Warehouse is identified with a particular time period, thus it gives access to the information from a historical point of view.
  • Non-Volatile - The previous data is not removed when new data is added to it. The Data Warehouse is kept separate from the operational database, therefore frequent changes in the operational database are not reflected in the Data Warehouse.
